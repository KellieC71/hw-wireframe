This was alarming because my father died at 60 yrs old from an anyeurism. They did alot of testing and came to the conclusino that I would need to get stents put in my brain to heal the anyeurisms. I was sent home and told to see an intevenious radiologist. I googled the stent procedure and found a Dr at Loyola Hospital in Chicago that saved a womens life by puting stents in her brain. So i called him. Dr. Ashely was super nice and super smart. He explained that if I did this procedure I would litterally save my life. He would have to do it twice tho. I had two anyeurisms on each side of my brain called mirror anyeurisms. So he said he could do the procesure in the next day or two. It was the scariest time of my life. I had never been put asleep for a procudure before so that was scary to me as well as having someone go into my vein at the groin all the way up to my brain. The procdure was over before I knew it.     What is flow diversion? Flow diversion is an endovascular technique whereby instead of placing a device inside the aneurysm sac, such as with coiling, the device is placed in the parent blood vessel to divert blood flow away from the aneurysm itself. How is flow diversion/Pipeline™ performed? Flow diversion slide Pipeline™ stent being placed During a flow-diversion/Pipeline™ procedure, a microcatheter is navigated past the aneurysm without having to enter the aneurysm. Then, the flow-diverting device (Pipeline™ Embolization Device) is deployed across the neck of the aneurysm in the parent blood vessel where the aneurysm is present. Almost immediately the blood flow to the aneurysm is reduced, and the complete closure of the aneurysm occurs between 6 weeks to 6 months after the procedure.
